Start with everyone

This is the baseline. Everything else is shown as a share of the same 100 people, so each change is comparable.

Voting age

Age is the first gate. It shows how much of the country is outside the voting universe before eligibility, registration, or documents.

Voting eligible

Voting age is not the same as voting eligible. This step narrows to the pool used in turnout analysis.

Registered voters

Registration is the operational reality. It is the group directly affected by registration rules and barriers.

People with a passport

This is not “out of eligible voters.” It is a share of the total population, including people who are not eligible to vote. That is intentional. It shows how common one commonly accepted citizenship document is in the country overall.

Name mismatch vs birth certificate

This is also out of total population, not out of eligible voters. It is about paperwork friction, not about eligibility or intent.

Voter fraud

If your story needs a fraud frame, keep it honest. Credible research finds illegal voting is extremely rare and nowhere near the scale required to affect national outcomes. In an “out of 100” view it rounds to essentially zero.
