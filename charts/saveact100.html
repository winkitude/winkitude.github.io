Start with everyone

This is the total U.S. population, distilled into 100 people.

Voting age

These are the people age 18 and older, old enough to vote.

Voting eligible

This narrows the picture to the voting eligible population, excluding people who are under 18, noncitizens, and others who are not legally eligible to vote.

Registered voters

These are registered voters out of the total population. This is the group already in the system and the one most directly affected by registration rules.

People with a passport

This is shown out of the total population, not out of eligible voters. It includes children and others who cannot vote. The point is to show how common a passport is as a document across the country overall.

Name mismatch vs birth certificate

This is also shown out of the total population. It is mostly about married women whose current name does not match their birth certificate, but other voters, including some trans voters, can run into similar documentation issues.

Voter fraud

The often-cited figure of 10 cases refers only to in-person voter impersonation in one News21 review from 2000 to 2012. Proven fraud cases across all categories are higher than that, but still tiny relative to the scale of U.S. elections and nowhere close to the population shown in this story.
