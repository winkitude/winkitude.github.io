Identity

Logo system

The no one media logo exists in two forms: the full wordmark ("no one media") and the short mark ("nom"). Use the full wordmark wherever space permits; the short mark is for avatars, favicons, watermarks, and small placements. The distressed texture is part of the mark — never recreate the wordmark in live type.

Full wordmark

Slate teal (on light) Default — use on white, cream, or light backgrounds ↓ SVG ↓ PNG

Clear space & minimum size

Minimum clear space
Clear zone = the height of the "n" on all four sides. Minimum size: 200px wide (full wordmark), 60px wide (short mark). Below those sizes the distressed texture starts to fill in.

Color system

The no one media palette is two teals, two clays, and a cream. Slate teal is the workhorse — it's the logo color and the only palette color dark enough for text. The clays are warm accents; cream is the default background. Contrast badges below are computed live against white.

Typography

Titles are set in Cal Sans; body copy is Nunito. Both are free via Google Fonts and work across web, video, and print. Cal Sans ships in a single display weight — use it for headlines and titles only, never for paragraphs. The wordmark itself is custom lettering with a distressed texture: never typeset "no one media" in live type as a logo substitute.

Usage

Imagery & icons

Imagery should feel warm, real, and human — documentary in spirit, never generic stock. Whether it's a thumbnail, a still, or a channel banner, center real people doing real things.

Photography & still principles

PrincipleGuidance
People-firstFeature real subjects, creators, and moments. Candid and in-context outperforms posed. Faces and hands at work make the strongest frames.
Documentary honestyStills should look like they came from the footage — because they did. Avoid overly staged compositions and clickbait-style exaggeration.
Warm, natural lightPrefer natural daylight or warm practical light. Avoid cold blue casts, harsh studio setups, or heavy filters. Skin tones should be accurate and warm.
Color harmonyImages with warm neutrals, earth tones, and muted teals sit best against the palette. The clays in the palette echo skin, wood, and daylight.
What to avoidGeneric stock photography, oversaturated thumbnail styling, heavy vignettes, low-res frames, AI-generated human imagery.

Text on images

Always use a dark scrim (60–70% opacity, slate-toned) when placing text over photography or video stills to ensure legibility.

Icons

Use simple line-style icons in slate teal or white. Warm clay may be used for a single highlighted icon or active state. Avoid filled, multi-color, or decorative icon styles — keep them clean and consistent with the mark.
