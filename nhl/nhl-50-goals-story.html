So you want to join the NHL's 50-goal club

A deep dive on the players who have achieved this elite milestone

Since the NHL’s first season in 1917-18, more than 8,000 players have laced up skates in the league. Only 99 of them have scored 50 goals in a single season.

I’ve had this data set in the back of my mind for years. Partly because it’s a great cross-section of NHL history. Partly because I always thought there was some version of the timeline where I could still make it happen. Sure, I can’t skate and haven’t exercised since before COVID. But Johnny Bucyk scored 51 at age 35. Ovechkin was still doing it at 37. The dream was alive.

It starts with Maurice “Rocket” Richard. In 1944-45, Richard became the first player to score 50 goals in a season, doing it in exactly 50 games. The NHL liked that enough to eventually name their annual goal-scoring trophy after him.

Context matters. Richard had 50 games. Later players got 70, then 78, then 80, eventually 82. He hit the mark with a shorter runway than almost anyone who came after him.

Games per NHL season, 1917–18 to 2024–25

Every season on this list is elite, but not the same kind of elite. Some guys had the best passer in the league feeding them all year. Some lived in front of the net tipping pucks they barely saw. Some just waited at the top of the circle and let them rip. A few look like flukes. Some never did it again. Some did it nine times.

However they got there, they did something most NHL players never will.

Pour one out

Pour one out for these talented losers. 49 goals is extremely impressive, but 49 may be the loneliest number in hockey. Some players were so close but couldn’t get that last tip in.

The Impossible: 50 in 50

Richard’s achievement created an even more exclusive club. He was the first of only five players to score 50 goals in 50 games or fewer. There’s an extended family of three more who completed the milestone in fewer than 50 personal games played — meaning they were injured or suspended for some games, but still hit 50 before the team played its 50th.

Defensemen Are Staying Home for This One

No defenseman has ever scored 50 goals in an NHL season. The closest anyone has come is Paul Coffey, who scored 48 in 1985–86. Bobby Orr topped out at 46. Doug Wilson hit 39. It is one of the few records in this sport that has never seriously been threatened, and given how the game has evolved defensively, it may never be.

The Era Effect

The 1980s were a different sport. High-flying, wide-open, and apparently populated entirely by players who had not heard of defensive zone coverage. Then came the dead puck era — big goalies with seemingly small five-holes, the trap shutting down the neutral zone, and scoring falling off a cliff. We’re over halfway through the 2020s and on pace to have the most 50-goal seasons since 2000.

When 50 goals happened

Not every era produced 50-goal scorers equally. The 1980s were a different sport — a wide-open, high-flying decade where 75 individual 50-goal seasons were recorded. Everything since has been a slow, grinding return toward respectability.

Seasons are assigned to the decade in which they occurred. The 1980s figure includes the extraordinary 1981–82 season, when Wayne Gretzky scored 92 goals — still the all-time record by a margin of 19. The 2020s are still in progress.

Data & Methodology

Season goal totals were compiled from publicly available NHL records and the Wikipedia dataset of players with 50-goal seasons. The dataset includes every player who recorded at least one 50-goal NHL season. Only NHL regular-season statistics are included.

Each ridgeline represents a player’s career scoring totals by season. The horizontal axis reflects the player's age at the start of the season (approximate). Peaks represent total goals scored that season. Black markers indicate seasons in which a player scored 50 or more goals.

Some early-career ages may be off by a year due to birthdate cutoffs and mid-season birthdays. The goal totals themselves reflect official NHL statistics.
